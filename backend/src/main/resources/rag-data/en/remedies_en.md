## Sore Throat

### 🚨 RED FLAGS (Emergency Triage)
*If the user reports any of the following, strictly advise immediate medical attention. Do not suggest home remedies.*
* Difficulty breathing or severe difficulty swallowing even their own saliva (drooling).
* Severe swelling on one side of the throat or neck.
* Sore throat accompanied by a high fever (over 101°F / 38.3°C).
* Symptoms lasting longer than a week without improvement.

### Description and Symptoms
A sore throat is pain, scratchiness, or irritation of the throat that often worsens when swallowing. It is most commonly caused by a viral infection (like a cold or the flu), but can also be due to bacterial infections (like strep throat) or environmental irritants.

### 🌿 Active Home Remedies

*   **Warm Salt Water Gargle**
    *   **Preparation:** Dissolve 1/2 teaspoon of table salt in a full glass (8 oz) of warm water.
    *   **Process:** Gargle the solution at the back of the throat for 30 seconds, then spit it out. Do not swallow it.
    *   **Timeline:** Repeat 3 to 4 times a day until the pain subsides.
    *   > **⚠️ Contraindication:** Not recommended for young children who have not yet learned how to gargle and spit properly.

*   **Golden Milk (Turmeric Milk)**
    *   **Preparation:** Mix 1/2 teaspoon of pure turmeric powder and a pinch of black pepper into a glass of warm milk.
    *   **Dosage:** Drink one glass before bedtime. Turmeric contains curcumin, a powerful anti-inflammatory, and the black pepper boosts its absorption.
    *   > **⚠️ Contraindication:** Avoid if lactose intolerant; substitute with almond milk or warm water.

| ✅ What to Eat (Pathya) | ❌ What to Avoid (Apathya) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Warm herbal teas (Chamomile, Ginger) | Highly acidic/citrus foods (can sting the throat) |
| Soft, easy-to-swallow foods (Oatmeal, mashed potatoes) | Hard, scratchy foods (Chips, crackers, crusty bread) |
| A spoonful of plain raw honey to coat the throat | Extremely spicy foods or hot sauces |

### 🛡️ Wellness and Prevention
*   Rest your voice. Talking loudly or excessively strains the inflamed vocal cords.
*   Use a cool-mist humidifier in your room at night to add moisture to the air and prevent the throat from drying out.
*   Change your toothbrush after you recover to avoid reinfection.

* Temperature exceeding 103°F (39.4°C) in adults, or lasting longer than 3 days.

* Fever in infants younger than 3 months exceeding 100.4°F (38°C).
* Fever accompanied by a skin rash that does not fade when pressed.

### Description and Symptoms
Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, usually indicating that the immune system is actively fighting off an infection (bacterial or viral).

### 🌿 Active Home Remedies

*   **Cold Compress (Tepid Sponging)**
    *   **Preparation:** Soak a clean cloth in tap water (room temperature). 
    *   **Process:** Wring out excess water and place it on the forehead, back of the neck, and underarms. Change the cloth every 5 minutes as it absorbs body heat.
    *   > **⚠️ Contraindication:** Never use ice-cold water or rubbing alcohol. Extreme cold causes shivering, which actually raises the internal core temperature.

*   **Electrolyte Hydration (ORS)**
    *   **Preparation:** Mix 1 liter of boiled and cooled water with 6 level teaspoons of sugar and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
    *   **Dosage:** Drink small sips continuously throughout the day to replace fluids lost through sweating.

    *   > **⚠️ Contraindication:** Do not use too much lemon juice or leave it on for extended periods, as its high acidity can irritate the scalp or lighten hair when exposed to sunlight.

*   **Aloe Vera Gel**
    *   **Preparation:** Extract fresh gel from an Aloe Vera leaf or use pure, store-bought Aloe Vera gel.
    *   **Process:** Apply directly to the scalp, let it sit for 30 minutes, and rinse thoroughly.

## Anemia and Weakness

Anemia occurs when your blood lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues. This results in chronic fatigue, generalized weakness, pale skin, cold hands and feet, and dizziness.

## Earache

### 🚨 RED FLAGS (Emergency Triage)

### Description and Symptoms
An earache is a sharp, dull, or burning pain in one or both ears. It is commonly caused by middle ear infections, fluid buildup behind the eardrum, impacted earwax, or changes in air pressure. Sometimes, pain from a toothache or jaw issue can radiate to the ear.

    *   **Process:** Using a sterile dropper, place 1 or 2 drops of the slightly warm oil into the ear canal. Keep the head tilted for a few minutes.

### 🛡️ Wellness and Prevention

## High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

### 🚨 RED FLAGS (Emergency Triage)

* Numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body (signs of a Stroke).
* Sudden blurred vision, confusion, or difficulty speaking.

### Description and Symptoms
High blood pressure (Hypertension) occurs when the force of the blood pushing against the walls of your arteries is consistently too high. It is often called the "silent killer" because it usually has no obvious symptoms until it reaches a severe or life-threatening stage.

    * > **⚠️ Contraindication:** Avoid taking raw garlic in large quantities if you are already on prescribed blood-thinning medications, as it can increase the risk of bleeding.

    * **Preparation:** Steep 1-2 dried hibiscus flowers (or a hibiscus tea bag) in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

### Description and Symptoms
Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. It occurs when your body doesn't produce enough insulin or can't use it effectively, causing sugar (glucose) to build up in your bloodstream. Symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, extreme hunger, unexplained weight loss, and fatigue.
